## Framework Validation

Not all interview advice is equal. Here's what the research actually supports.

| Framework | Status | Tier |
|-----------|--------|------|
| STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) | Validated. Past-behavior questions have higher criterion validity than hypotheticals | T1 |
| Structured interviews | 2x more predictive of job performance than unstructured | T1 |
| SOAR, CAR, PAR | No data on comparative efficacy. STAR remains the dominant standard | Gap |

### Using STAR Effectively

The most common mistake is making STAR answers too long. Structure:
- **Situation**: 1-2 sentences. Set the context. Don't over-explain.
- **Task**: 1 sentence. What was your specific responsibility?
- **Action**: 2-3 sentences. What did YOU do? (Not the team — you.)
- **Result**: 1-2 sentences. What changed? Quantify if possible.

Total: 60-90 seconds spoken. Practice with a timer.

### Generating Predicted Questions

When preparing for a specific interview, generate questions in 3 categories:

**1. Role-specific behavioral** (from the JD)
Map each listed responsibility to a STAR story. "Tell me about a time you [did the thing in the JD]."

**2. Company-specific situational**
Use research pre-flight findings. "How would you approach [specific challenge the company faces]?"

**3. Values and culture fit**
From the company's stated values or Glassdoor reviews. "Describe a time when you had to [embody stated value] under pressure."

Generate 15 predicted questions minimum for thorough prep.

## AI Interview Systems

Two major platforms dominate. Both are beatable with preparation.

### HireVue

| What it measures | Details | Tier |
|-----------------|---------|------|
| Data points per interview | 25,000 | T2 |
| Dimensions | Response timing, verbal fluency, facial expressions, answer completeness | T2 |

**How to prepare:**
- Practice answering on camera in the same room you'll use
- Speak at natural pace — rushing degrades fluency scores
- Look at the camera (not the screen) for eye contact signal
- Structure answers with STAR — the system scores completeness
- Don't read from notes — it detects reduced facial engagement

### Pymetrics

| What it measures | Details | Tier |
|-----------------|---------|------|
| Games | 12 neuroscience-based games | T2 |
| Dimensions | Focus, Risk Tolerance, Memory, Empathy, Fairness, Impulse Control | T2 |

**How to prepare:**
- Sleep 7-9 hours the night before — fatigue degrades measured traits [T2]
- Do cognitive reflex training (Lumosity/NeuroNation) for one week prior [T2]
- Practice in simulated conditions: quiet room, full screen, 25-minute blocks [T2]
- Do NOT try to "game" the games — algorithms detect inconsistency [T2]
- If doing both Pymetrics and a behavioral interview, ensure your interview answers align with the traits the games measured

### General AI Interview Prep

- Test your setup (camera, mic, lighting, background) 24 hours before
- Record yourself answering 3 practice questions and review
- Ensure your background is clean and well-lit — visual noise adds cognitive load for both AI and human reviewers

## Salary Negotiation

Three frameworks, each with a different strength.

### Chris Voss — Tactical Empathy [T2]

Best for: any negotiation where you need to build rapport first.

| Technique | How to use it |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Mirroring** | Repeat the last 1-3 words of what they said. Creates rapport and prompts elaboration. |
| **Labeling** | "It seems like the budget is tight for this level." Names the dynamic without confrontation. |
| **Calibrated questions** | "How am I supposed to make that work?" Turns pressure back without aggression. |

### Ramit Sethi — Briefcase Technique [T2]

Best for: when you can demonstrate specific value before the number comes up.

Prepare a 1-3 page document showing:
1. Three specific problems you've identified in their business
2. Your proposed approach to solving each one
3. Evidence from your past results (quantified)

Present it during the conversation: "I put together some thoughts on how I'd approach the first 90 days." This anchors the discussion in value, not just salary history.

### Academic — ZOPA/BATNA [T1]

Best for: when you have competing offers or clear market data.

| Concept | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **ZOPA** | Zone of Possible Agreement — the overlap between what they'll pay and what you'll accept |
| **BATNA** | Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement — your walkaway option |

Know your BATNA before entering any negotiation. A strong BATNA (another offer, current job you're willing to keep) gives you real leverage. Without one, you're guessing.

### Negotiation Principles
- Never give a number first if you can avoid it
- When you must anchor, anchor high with justification (market data from Levels.fyi or BLS — see market-data.md)
- Negotiate total compensation, not just base: equity, signing bonus, remote flexibility, PTO, professional development budget
- Get the final offer in writing before accepting verbally

## Memorable Candidate Questions

Questions that differentiate you from every other candidate who asks "what's the team culture like?"

These patterns are documented as effective by hiring manager surveys [T2]:

**Firm-specific** (requires research):
- "How does this team reconcile high-speed delivery with [company's stated value]?"
- "What was the catalyst for the last major project failure, and how did recovery change your workflow?"

**KPI-focused**:
- "If I excelled for six months, what specific KPIs would have moved most?"
- "What does the difference between good and great look like in this role's first year?"

**Values-driven**:
- "What kind of work environment brings out the best in you as a leader?"
- "What's something about this team that wouldn't show up in the job description?"

Pattern: questions that show you've done your homework and are evaluating them too. Never ask questions that are answered on their website.

## Mock Interview Formats

| Format | Best for | Tier |
|--------|----------|------|
| Professional mock interview (career coach or mentor) | Highest value for blind spot identification | T2 |
| Peer mock interview (friend in same industry) | Good for technical practice, lower stakes | T2 |
| Self-recorded (camera + timer) | Minimum viable — reveals filler words and pacing | T3 |

Recommendation: at least one mock interview with someone who will give honest feedback before any high-stakes interview. Friends are supportive but often not critical enough.

## Follow-Up Messages

| Finding | Tier |
|---------|------|
| 74% of hiring managers report higher satisfaction when candidates follow up | T2 |
| No causal evidence that follow-up changes hiring outcomes | T2 |
| Best timing: by lunchtime the next business day | T2 |

**What this means**: Send a follow-up because it's professional practice and keeps you on their radar. Don't expect it to rescue a bad interview. Keep it short: thank them, reference one specific thing discussed, reaffirm interest.

Do NOT send follow-up through multiple channels (email + LinkedIn + text). One message, one channel.
